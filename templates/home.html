About Lung Cancer

Lung cancer is a malignant tumor characterized by uncontrolled cell growth in tissues of the lung. If left untreated, this growth can spread beyond the lung into nearby tissue or other parts of the body. The majority of lung cancer cases are linked to smoking, though non-smokers can also develop the disease due to factors like exposure to radon gas, secondhand smoke, air pollution, or genetic predispositions.

Common symptoms include persistent coughing, chest pain, shortness of breath, wheezing, coughing up blood, and unexplained weight loss. Because symptoms often don't appear until the disease is advanced, early detection is critical. Treatment options include surgery, radiation therapy, chemotherapy, targeted drug therapy, and immunotherapy.

Our Prediction System

Our Lung Cancer Prediction System is a web-based platform designed to support early detection of lung cancer by analyzing user input data such as age, smoking habits, shortness of breath, fatigue, and other relevant medical conditions. By leveraging machine learning models trained on real medical datasets, the system evaluates patterns and indicators to provide a probability estimate of lung cancer presence.

This system is not intended to replace professional medical diagnosis, but rather to assist users in making informed decisions and encourage them to seek further evaluation if a high risk is detected. The tool is especially useful in regions with limited access to advanced medical diagnostics.
